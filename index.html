Nariyoshi Kesuke Miyagi
(Japanese 宮城成義)

Sensei, Mentor, Friend

Here are some major milestones in Mr. Miyagi's life:

  • Mr Miyagi was born June 9, 1925 in Okinawa, Japan.
  • In 1943, to avoid a fight to the death with his best friend, Mr. Miyagi left Okinawa and emigrated to the United States.
    Arriving in Hawaii, he began working as a farm labourer for a short time, where he also met his wife, before they both moved to Los Angeles, where he would graduate from the Santa Barbara University of California.
  • 1944 - Volunteered for the US Army during World War II and was drafted to the 442nd Regimental Combat Team. He was primarily stationed in Italy and fought in the Battle of Anzio.
  • Sadly, while away in Europe, Mr. Miyagi suffered his greatest personal tragedy of his life. His wife and their son passed away in Manzanar internment camp in November 1944 due to complications during childbirth. He would never remarry.
  • Over the course of his military service, Mr. Miyagi was promoted to the rank of Staff Sergeant and was highly-decorated, having earned not only the Medal of Honor, but also the Silver Star, Bronze Star, Army Commendation Medal, Purple Heart, Presidential Unit Citation, Army Good Conduct Medal, American Campaign Medal, European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal, World War II Victory Medal, Combat Infantryman Badge and the 442nd Regimental Combat Team Service Identification Badge.
  • Following relief from the military, Mr. Miyagi entered into the profession of maintenance man and built his garden, something he was proud of and continued to work on until he passed away. He also spent time restoring a collection of classic cars.
  • In 1984 he founded Miyagi-Do Karate, a Karate Dojo based in Los Angeles.
  • That same year, his student, Daniel LaRusso would go on to win the All Valley Karate Tournament.
  • Six months after the tournament, both would travel back to Okinawa as Mr. Miyagi's father was in ill-health. His father would pass during that visit.
    Miyagi would go-on to make amends with his former best friend after saving his life during a typhoon.
  • 1985 saw him open a Bonsai store.
    Also, after initial reluctance to defend the title won in the previous year, he would help Daniel to win the All Valley Karate Tournament for a second time.
  • In the spring of 1994 he would travel to Boston to attend the fiftieth anniversary of the 442nd Infantry.
  • Mr. Miyagi passed away on November 15, 2011
